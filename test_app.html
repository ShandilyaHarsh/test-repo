We often express ourselves the way we want people to see us, and not the real us. There is nothing wrong with it, but for one you should always be able to differentiate between you and YOU. The Long


The place you are born at, the people you are associated with, the city you live in, the roads you walk on, the windows you peek through- Each and every thing you do is your identity. For so long, we have limited the definition of identity to the bare minimum of physical self that we have forgotten the real meaning of it. When people go through depression and mental issues, they often confuse their identity with "null". Their mind underestimates its own value and succumbs to the pressure created by the situation. Rather, at that time you should understand the real YOU, the You which you have hidden from everyone, the you which only YOU know about. You should think of the people who care about you, your parents, your friends or that stray dog you feed regularly or the ant which feeds on the sugar particles which you intentionally drop. We often overlook the small things and depress ourselves, but the minute things are the ones which when augmented become the strongest force to go against. There is no point reading this paragraph if you do notice the small things, if you do notice the smile on your mother's face when she sees your childhood picture, if you do notice all the little things which make a difference. If you don't, you should know the absence of those small things won't matter, but YOUR ABSENCE WILL. You do make a difference, however small, however big.
